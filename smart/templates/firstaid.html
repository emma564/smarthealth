Broken Bone treatment

Key Skills

  • Encourage the person to support the injury with their hand, or use a cushion or items of clothing to prevent unnecessary movement.
  • As soon as possible, call your medical emergence number or get someone else to do it.
  • Continue supporting the injury until help arrives.

Choking Treatment

Key Skills

  • Hit them firmly on their back between the shoulder blades to dislodge the object.
  • If necessary, see the doctor.

Unresponsive and not breathing

Key Skills

  • Check breathing by tilting their head backwards and looking and feeling for breaths.
  • Call your medical emergence as soon as possible, or get someone else to do it.
  • Push firmly downwards in the middle of the chest and then release.
  • Push at a regular rate until help arrives.

Strains Treatment

Key Skills

  • Get the person to rest.
  • Apply an ice pack to the injury.
  • If there is no improvement seek medical advice.

Burn Treatment

Key Skills

  • Cool the burn under cold running water for at least ten minutes.
  • After the burn has been cooled, cover it with cling film or a clean plastic bag.
  • If necessary, call your medical emergence number or get someone else to do it.

Allergic Treatment

Key Skills

  • The person may develop a rash, itchiness or swelling on their hands, feet or face. Their breathing may slow down.
  • Call your medical emergence number when you observe these symptoms.
  • Give constant reassurance until help arrives.

Head Inquiry Treatment

Key Skills

  • Ask them to rest and apply something cold to the injury (e.g. frozen vegetables wrapped in a tea towel).
  • If they become drowsy or vomit, call your medical emergence number or get someone else to do it.

Heart Attack Treatment

Key Shills

  • The person may have persistent, vice-like chest pain, which may spread to their arms, neck, jaw, back or stomach.
  • As soon as possible, call your medical emergence number or get someone else to do it.
  • Make sure they are in a position that is comfortable for them (e.g. sit them on the floor, leaning against a wall or chair).
  • Give them constant reassurance while waiting for the ambulance.

Stroke Treatment

Key Skills

  • Think F.A.S.T. Face: is there weakness on one side of their face? Arms: can they raise both arms? Speech: is their speech easily understood? Time:Call for medical help.
